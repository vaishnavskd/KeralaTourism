Districts

Kerala, is divided into 14 administrative districts. Each district has its own unique cultural, historical, and geographical features.

To know more, Click Here

Thiruvananthapuram

Known for its historical landmarks, vibrant culture, and beautiful beaches.

Kollam

Known for its scenic backwaters and historic significance, also an important trade and industrial hub.

Pathanamthitta

Known for its religious diversity and numerous Hindu temples.

Alappuzha

Known as the "Venice of the East".

Kottayam

Characterized by its picturesque landscapes, rubber plantations, and educational institutions.

Idukki

Known for its dense forests, tea and spice plantations, and the majestic Idukki Arch Dam, an engineering marvel.

Ernakulam

Known for modernity and tradition, with vibrant markets, historic landmarks, and a thriving port.

Thrissur

Known as the "Cultural Capital of Kerala".

Palakkad

Known for its lush green paddy fields, Palakkad is often referred to as the "Granary of Kerala."

Malappuram

Known for its historical significance, Islamic heritage, and educational institutions.

Kozhikode

Known for its historical importance as a major trade center during ancient times.

Wayanad

Known for its lush green forests, wildlife sanctuaries, and tea plantations.

Kannur

Known for its beautiful beaches, historic forts, and handloom industry. It has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its folk art forms like Theyyam.

Kasaragod

Known for its serene beaches, historic forts, and the influence of the Tulu culture.
